# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Small business owners who want an online presence but need more than just a website. They're founders, solo operators, and growing teams who know they need to be online but are overwhelmed by options. When they land on this site, they should feel immediate confidence: "This person understands what I actually need." They arrive curious or skeptical; they should leave convinced. Not sold by hype, but by visible craft and clear thinking.

Secondary users: other developers and designers evaluating template purchases from the Design Lab.

## Product Purpose

Nimbus Forma Studio is a creative builder's portfolio and product storefront. It serves two functions:

1. **Portfolio** — Showcase Dee's work, philosophy, and process to convert visitors into clients. The site itself is the proof of capability. Every interaction, animation, and layout decision demonstrates what hiring Nimbus means.

2. **Design Lab** — Sell website templates (Studio Noir, Elevate, Pop Store, Vitalis, Roast, Mono) as standalone products. Each template is a live, browsable multi-page demo that proves its value before purchase.

Success looks like: a small business owner visits, sees the quality gap between this and every other freelancer site they've browsed, and books a call. Or a developer sees a template, clicks through the live preview, and buys.

## Brand Personality

**Intentional. Crafted. Human.**

Not a studio. Not an agency. A creative builder. "We build what doesn't exist yet." The work isn't just design and code; it's business development, SOPs, conversion strategy, and systems thinking.

Voice: Direct, confident, zero fluff. Speaks like a builder who respects your time. No buzzwords, no "synergy," no "leverage." Says what it does, does what it says.

Emotional goal: The visitor should feel that every pixel was placed with purpose. The slogan "Built with intention" isn't marketing; it's a verifiable claim demonstrated by the site itself.

Three pillars: Craft + Systems + Intelligence.

## Anti-references

- Generic freelancer portfolios with stock photos and "I'm passionate about design" copy
- SaaS-cream aesthetic (off-white backgrounds, soft shadows, rounded everything)
- Template marketplace sites that look like they were built from their own templates
- Over-animated sites where motion is decoration, not communication
- Agency sites with team grids, client logo bars, and "our process" timelines that all look the same

## Design Principles

1. **Practice what you preach.** The portfolio site must be the best work in the portfolio. If a section looks like it came from a template, it fails the brand promise.

2. **Show, don't tell.** Don't write "we build fast websites" — build a fast website. Don't write "we care about details" — show details that nobody asked for but everyone notices.

3. **Earn every element.** Nothing decorative. No corner marks, no floating dots, no gradients that exist because the space felt empty. If an element doesn't communicate, remove it.

4. **Conviction over consensus.** Make strong design choices. Dark because the work is the light source, not because "dark mode is trendy." Purple because it's distinctive, not because it tested well.

5. **Respect the visitor's intelligence.** No "scroll down to learn more." No progress bars that fake engagement. Trust that good work holds attention without tricks.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG AA minimum for all text contrast
- Reduced motion support (prefers-reduced-motion) on all animations — already implemented
- Keyboard navigable with visible focus indicators
- Skip-to-content link present
- No content hidden behind hover-only interactions on mobile
- Color not used as the sole indicator of state
